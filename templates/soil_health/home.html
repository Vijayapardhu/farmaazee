Soil Health Tips
Test Soil Regularly

Test your soil every 2-3 years to monitor nutrient levels and pH balance.

Practice Crop Rotation

Rotate crops to prevent nutrient depletion and reduce pest pressure.

Maintain Proper Moisture

Ensure adequate soil moisture without overwatering to prevent erosion.

Add Organic Matter

Incorporate compost and organic materials to improve soil structure.

Common Soil Types
Clay Soil

Heavy, retains water well, good for rice and wheat

Loamy Soil

Ideal soil type, balanced nutrients, good for most crops

Sandy Soil

Light, drains quickly, good for root vegetables

Essential Nutrients
Nitrogen (N)

Essential for leaf growth and chlorophyll production

Phosphorus (P)

Important for root development and flowering

Potassium (K)

Helps with disease resistance and fruit quality
